Pope Leo XIV Issues 42,000-Word AI Encyclical 3 newsletters

Pope Leo XIV published "Magnifica Humanitas," his first encyclical, devoting nearly the entire 83-page document to artificial intelligence. Leo warned that AI risks becoming a "new Tower of Babel," called for it to be "disarmed" before it dominates humanity, and demanded that lethal decisions never be delegated to algorithms. He criticized the concentration of AI power among private transnational companies that "already surpass the capacity of many governments," and urged robust independent oversight and regulation. In a striking gesture, Anthropic co-founder Christopher Olah stood beside Leo at the public presentation, signaling an unusual Vatican-Silicon Valley dialogue on AI safety. The encyclical was signed May 15, the 135th anniversary of Leo XIII's landmark labor encyclical on workers' rights.

Teen Summer Jobs Are Vanishing 2 newsletters

Teen summer employment is on track for its lowest level in decades.
