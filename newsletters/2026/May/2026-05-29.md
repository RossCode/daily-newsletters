## Top Stories

### [Anthropic Overtakes OpenAI: Opus 4.8 and a $965B Valuation](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-opus-4-8)

*(4 newsletters)*

Anthropic closed a $65B Series H led by Altimeter, Dragoneer, Greenoaks, and Sequoia, pushing its valuation to $965B and making it the most valuable AI lab in the world. On the same day, they released Claude Opus 4.8, which outperforms GPT-5.5 and Gemini 3.1 Pro on agentic coding, computer use, financial analysis, and Humanity's Last Exam. The model is notably more honest, designed to flag its own uncertainties, comes at the same price as 4.7, and includes a 3x cheaper Fast mode. A "Mythos-class" follow-up is coming within weeks.

### [Anthropic and OpenAI Have Found Enterprise Product-Market Fit](https://simonwillison.net/2026/May/27/product-market-fit/)

*(3 newsletters)*

A widely shared Simon Willison analysis argues that both labs have crossed the PMF threshold with coding and general-purpose agent tools. The evidence: enterprises now spend over $200 per user per month, usage-based pricing is replacing flat subscriptions, and coding agents are amplifying that spend significantly. TLDR Dev and TLDR AI both picked it up the same day, signaling clear resonance across the technical community.

### [Apple to Overhaul Siri and iOS 27 at WWDC June 8](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-05-28/apple-ios-27-photos-screenshots-revamped-siri-pro-camera-app-new-ai-features)

*(2 newsletters)*

Bloomberg previewed iOS 27: a revamped Siri interface and a new ChatGPT-style standalone app. WWDC is June 8 and the launch is framed as pivotal for Apple's AI strategy after years of delays. It is also expected to be Tim Cook's final major product launch before handing leadership to John Ternus.

### [Biohub Drops an Open "World Model" of Protein Biology](https://biohub.ai/esm/protein/about)

*(2 newsletters)*

Backed by Zuckerberg and Chan's CZI, Biohub released ESMFold2, a protein structure prediction model trained on 2.8 billion sequences that claims to outperform AlphaFold on both protein-protein and antibody-antigen prediction. ESM Atlas maps 6.8 billion sequences and 1.1 billion predicted structures. Early lab results show 36 to 88% hit rates against five cancer and immune disease targets. Everything is open source.

### [Claude Code Gets Dynamic Workflows: Parallel Subagents for Complex Tasks](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-code-dynamic-workflows)

*(2 newsletters)*

Anthropic shipped Dynamic Workflows in Claude Code, allowing Claude to write orchestration scripts that spin up hundreds of parallel subagents to tackle large, complex tasks end-to-end. Available now in research preview on the CLI, Desktop, and VS Code extension for Max, Team, and Enterprise plans. Note: it can consume significantly more tokens than a typical session.

### [Waymo's Ojai Robotaxi Now Accepting Riders in SF, LA, Phoenix](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/28/waymos-newest-robotaxi-is-chinese-made-built-to-make-money-and-now-accepting-riders/)

*(2 newsletters)*

Waymo's new Ojai vehicle, a modified Zeekr-built electric minivan, is designed to lower costs and withstand commercial-scale wear-and-tear. Select riders in San Francisco, Los Angeles, and Phoenix can try it now, with San Diego, Las Vegas, and Denver coming this summer. Waymo plans to have thousands of Ojai vehicles on the road by end of year.

### [ElevenLabs Music v2 Launches](https://elevenlabs.io/music)

*(2 newsletters)*

Major upgrade to ElevenLabs' music generation: sharper vocals, multilingual range, genre-bending mid-track control, and precise inpainting so you can regenerate any section without touching the rest. Handles fast rap, opera-to-metal transitions, and sound-effect layering. Two separate AI newsletters flagged the release.

## Also Worth Knowing

- **[CEO Confidence Craters.](https://conference-board.org/)** The Conference Board Q2 survey of 141 Fortune 500 CEOs returned a confidence score of 47, down 12 points from Q1. Only 8% said conditions worsened in Q1; 47% said so in Q2. Consumer confidence and University of Michigan sentiment are also at multi-year lows.

- **[Asana Acquires Stack AI.](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/28/asana-acquires-no-code-agent-builder-stack-ai/)** Asana bought the no-code AI agent builder as part of its push to become an AI-native workplace platform. Stack AI founders Tony Rosinol and Bernard Aceituno are joining Asana. Financial terms not disclosed.

- **[Samsung Workers Lock In $370K AI Bonuses.](https://finance.yahoo.com/economy/policy/articles/samsung-workers-wrap-vote-massive-211050339.html)** About 78,000 Samsung workers approved a labor deal making them eligible for annual bonuses of around $370K, tied to operating profit that was up roughly 750% in Q1 on AI memory demand.

- **[Sam Altman Walks Back AI Job-Loss Warnings.](https://time.com/article/2026/05/26/sam-altman-ai-job-losses-openAI-/)** Ahead of an expected Q4 2026 IPO at roughly $1T valuation, OpenAI CEO Altman told Time he was "pretty wrong" about AI's near-term labor market impact and now believes the human side of work can't be replaced.

- **AI Money Is Reshaping the Housing Market.** Luxury home prices in the SF Bay Area rose 13.4% since ChatGPT launched in 2022; SF median house price hit $2.15M in March. Non-luxury prices in the same market fell 3.8%. Median SF one-bedroom rent jumped over 21% in the last year to above $4,000.

- **Blue Origin New Glenn Explodes During Static Fire Test.** A spectacular explosion occurred during a static fire test in Florida. No injuries; cause is under investigation.

- **Tesla Optimus Factory Construction Begins at Giga Texas.** Tesla is adding 5.2 million square feet to Gigafactory Texas for Optimus production. Target: 27,000 robots per day; production starts July or August, high-volume by Summer 2027.
