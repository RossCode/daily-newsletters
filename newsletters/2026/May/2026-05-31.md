## Top Stories

### [Claude Opus 4.8 Launches With a Focus on Honesty, Not Just Power](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-opus-4-8)

*(2 newsletters)*

Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.8 launched at the same price as 4.7, and the headline feature isn't speed or context length -- it's calibrated uncertainty. Early testers report the model now actively flags its own doubts, breaks down where confidence drops, and refuses to fake an answer it can't verify. Anthropic says it's four times less likely to let flawed code pass without comment. The move is a deliberate bet on enterprise deployability at a time when hallucinations remain the top reason companies pull agents out of production -- and the flat pricing makes the upgrade easy to justify.

### [AI Is Reshuffling Jobs: ClickUp Cuts 22% While AI-Skilled Openings Hit a 12-Month High](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/25/what-clickups-mass-layoff-tells-us-about-the-future-of-work/)

*(2 newsletters)*

ClickUp announced it laid off 22% of its workforce this week, citing a "radical embrace of AI" after deploying roughly 3,000 internal AI agents. That same week, AI-skilled job postings hit their highest level in a year. Companies cut 15,000+ roles and called it an "AI pivot" while simultaneously posting record demand for workers who can build and operate AI systems. The labor market isn't shrinking -- it's sorting fast.

- **[Small Towns Are Beating NYC and SF in Population Growth.](https://thehustle.co/originals/why-so-many-americans-moved-to-the-middle-of-nowhere)** New US Census data shows the 25 largest US cities grew just 1.3% from 2020 to 2025, vs. 3.1% nationally. NYC, Boston, and LA all lost residents. Meanwhile, exurbs like Fulshear, TX (300% growth) and Buckeye, AZ are adding more people numerically than almost any major metro. Remote work, housing costs, and a preference for larger homes are driving the shift.

- **[Shift AI: Free Home Cleaning in Exchange for Robot Training Data.](https://www.shiftapp.nyc/)** The startup sends a vetted cleaner to your home at no charge -- but the cleaner wears a camera, and the footage trains the next generation of humanoid robots.
