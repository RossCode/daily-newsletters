## Top Stories

### [OpenAI and Anthropic Both Launch PE-Backed Enterprise AI Ventures](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/04/anthropic-and-openai-are-both-launching-joint-ventures-for-enterprise-ai-services/)

*(6 newsletters)*

On the same day, the two leading AI labs announced parallel private equity distribution plays. Anthropic closed a $1.5B joint venture with Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, and Goldman Sachs to embed Claude into mid-market companies, while OpenAI finalized "The Deployment Company," raising $4B from 19 investors including TPG, Brookfield, Bain, and SoftBank at a $10B valuation. The shared thesis: PE firms already own hundreds of portfolio companies that lack the in-house talent to deploy AI, so one check buys access to the whole portfolio. Blackstone alone controls 250+ businesses; across all investors involved, the count runs into the thousands. This marks a fundamental shift in AI distribution, away from traditional enterprise sales and toward PE-mediated deployment at scale.

### [GameStop Makes $56B Unsolicited Bid to Acquire eBay](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/04/gamestop-ebay-takeover-bid-ryan-cohen-gaming-retail-ecommerce.html)

*(3 newsletters)*

GameStop CEO Ryan Cohen launched a surprise offer to buy eBay for $56B, pitching a combined physical-and-digital platform as a "legitimate Amazon competitor." The offer values eBay at $125/share (a 20% premium), but markets were skeptical: eBay shares climbed only to $109 while GameStop stock fell 10%. The math is audacious: GameStop is a $12B company swinging at one worth four times its size, with no publicly disclosed financing plan. Cohen's vision pairs GameStop's collector retail footprint with eBay's 130 million active buyers to build a serious secondhand marketplace force.

### [Anthropic Co-founder: AI Will Build Its Own Successors by 2028](https://importai.substack.com/p/import-ai-455-automating-ai-research)

*(3 newsletters)*

Anthropic co-founder Jack Clark published a widely-read essay putting 60%+ odds on AI systems training their own successors before 2029. He backed the prediction with public benchmark data: AI independent work capability has jumped from 30-second tasks in 2022 to 12-hour autonomous runs in 2026, with 100-hour runs projected by year-end. On the SWE-Bench coding benchmark, performance leaped from 2% (Claude 2) to 93.9% (Mythos Preview) in under three years. OpenAI has separately stated its goal of a fully automated research intern by September 2026.

### [Musk v. Altman Trial: Settlement Texts, Distillation Admission, and $30B in Shares](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/04/musk-altman-open-ai-settlement-trial-brockman.html)

*(3 newsletters)*

The first week of the Elon Musk v. Sam Altman trial in Oakland produced several notable moments. Musk admitted under cross-examination that xAI "partly" used OpenAI's models to train its own, an admission his team downplayed as "standard practice." Pre-trial texts revealed Musk messaged Greg Brockman about a potential settlement two days before proceedings began; when Brockman suggested both sides drop all claims, Musk replied: "By the end of this week, you and Sam will be the most hated men in America." Week two opened with Brockman confirming OpenAI is exploring an IPO at its $850B valuation and disclosing he holds nearly $30B in OpenAI shares.

## Also Worth Knowing

- **[GitHub Is Buckling Under AI Agent Load.](https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/p/the-pulse-github-breaks)** HashiCorp founder Mitchell Hashimoto logged every GitHub outage for a month, then quit the platform after 18 years, calling it "no longer a place for serious work." GitHub's CTO acknowledged the platform wasn't built for agent-scale traffic, but Vercel, Sentry, and Railway handle the same load fine, suggesting the real issue is two decades of tech debt and a half-finished Azure migration.

- **[Vercel Launches DeepSec, an AI-Powered Security Scanner.](https://vercel.com/blog/introducing-deepsec-find-and-fix-vulnerabilities-in-your-code-base)** The open-source tool deploys coding agents to find vulnerabilities across large codebases, running locally against developers' existing Claude or Codex setups and scaling to thousands of parallel sandboxes. It traces data flows to reduce false positives and exports findings as ready-to-fix engineering tickets.

- **[Peter Thiel Backs Ocean-Based AI Data Centers.](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260504552400/en/Panthalassa-Raises-140-Million-to-Power-AI-at-Sea)** Panthalassa raised $140M in a Thiel-led Series B for autonomous floating compute nodes that convert wave energy into power for AI chips, cooled by seawater, connected via Starlink. Valued near $1B, the company plans commercial deployment in 2027 as land-based data center construction faces growing public opposition.

- **[DeepSeek Previews V4, Claiming Near-Frontier Performance.](https://lastweekin.ai/p/last-week-in-ai-340-openai-vs-musk)** DeepSeek released preview versions of V4 Flash and V4 Pro, both text-only mixture-of-experts models with 1 million-token context windows, open-sourced on Hugging Face. V4 Pro packs 1.6 trillion total parameters (49B active), with coding and reasoning benchmarks approaching frontier Western models.

- **[Tesla Hits 10B FSD Miles, the Threshold Musk Set for Unsupervised Autonomy.](https://www.theverge.com/transportation/922900/tesla-10-billion-miles-unsupervised-fsd-robotaxi-elon-musk)** Tesla's fleet surpassed the data milestone Musk designated for "safe unsupervised" operation, but the system still requires a human in the seat and regulators have not approved a commercial robotaxi rollout. Waymo is already operating driverless rides in 10 cities.

- **[White House Weighs Pre-Release Vetting of AI Models.](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/05/04/technology/trump-ai-models.html)** The Trump administration is considering an executive order requiring government review before public deployment, citing cybersecurity risks, job displacement, and China competition. Separately, the Pentagon awarded classified-network AI contracts to OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, AWS, Nvidia, SpaceX, and Reflection AI, with Anthropic excluded under a supply chain risk designation.

- **[AI Outperformed Doctors in Harvard Emergency Room Study.](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2026/apr/30/ai-outperforms-doctors-in-harvard-trial-of-emergency-triage-diagnoses)** AI correctly diagnosed emergency patients in 67% of cases, compared to 50-55% for human doctors, adding to a growing body of evidence on AI performance in high-stakes clinical triage.

- **[Sierra Raises $950M at $15.8B Valuation.](https://qz.com/sierra-ai-funding-round-950-million-valuation-050426)** The AI customer experience platform, which says it now serves over 40% of Fortune 50 companies, raised the round led by Tiger Global and Google Ventures with an eye toward a future IPO.
