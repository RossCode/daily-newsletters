## Top Stories

### [Pope Leo XIV Issues 42,000-Word AI Encyclical](https://www.vatican.va/content/leo-xiv/en/encyclicals/documents/20260515-magnifica-humanitas.html)

*(3 newsletters)*

Pope Leo XIV published "Magnifica Humanitas," his first encyclical, devoting nearly the entire 83-page document to artificial intelligence. Leo warned that AI risks becoming a "new Tower of Babel," called for it to be "disarmed" before it dominates humanity, and demanded that lethal decisions never be delegated to algorithms. He criticized the concentration of AI power among private transnational companies that "already surpass the capacity of many governments," and urged robust independent oversight and regulation. In a striking gesture, Anthropic co-founder Christopher Olah stood beside Leo at the public presentation, signaling an unusual Vatican-Silicon Valley dialogue on AI safety. The encyclical was signed May 15, the 135th anniversary of Leo XIII's landmark labor encyclical on workers' rights.

### [Teen Summer Jobs Are Vanishing](https://www.wsj.com/articles/teen-summer-jobs)

*(2 newsletters)*

Teen summer employment is on track for its lowest level in decades. Outplacement firm Challenger, Gray & Christmas projects just 790,000 seasonal jobs for teens this summer, down from over 1 million two years ago. AI-driven automation and older workers delaying retirement are squeezing teens out of traditional entry-level roles, while hiring in entertainment and leisure is down 70% year-over-year. Some teens are adapting by turning to gig economy platforms: Uber and DoorDash report Gen Z registrations are up 8.4% year over year.

### [Microsoft Quietly Cancels Most Claude Code Licenses](https://thenextweb.com/news/microsoft-claude-code-retreat-ai-cost)

*(2 newsletters)*

Microsoft is canceling most direct Claude Code licenses within its Experiences and Devices group, telling affected engineers to migrate to GitHub Copilot CLI by June 30. The move reveals a growing tension in enterprise AI: at current token prices, the unit economics for AI coding tools do not pencil out at scale. Expect similar scrutiny industry-wide if pricing does not compress significantly.
